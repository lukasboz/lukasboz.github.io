My Journey

Building with purpose

My path into software started with a curiosity about how computers actually work -- not just writing code, but understanding the hardware underneath. That curiosity led me to Computer Science at Western University, where I've maintained a 3.8 GPA and made the Dean's Honours List twice.

I'm currently at AMD, working on computer vision, machine learning, and NPU driver validation. I've built agentic AI development pipelines, engineered CI infrastructure for driver testing, and helped maintain 99% uptime across 300+ workstations. Before AMD, I was at J.D. Power building QA automation frameworks with Playwright and TypeScript, integrating CI/CD pipelines with GitLab and Xray, and improving test reporting with Allure.

Outside of work, I build projects that push my skills -- from a C++ tensor computation library with CUDA acceleration to facial recognition systems with OpenCV. I believe the best software happens at the intersection of performance and usability.
